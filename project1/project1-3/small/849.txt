  "{{short description|Czech music reviewer, composer and musicologist}} \n {{For|the comic strip cartoonist|Ambr\u00f3s}} \n [[Image:Ambros August Wilhelm 1862.jpg|right|thumb]] \n '''August Wilhelm Ambros''' (17 November 1816{{snd}}28 June 1876) < ref name=Blom > [[Eric Blom|Blom, Eric]] (2005) ''Everyman's Dictionary of Music'', Kessinger Publishing. p. 15. {{ISBN|1-4179-8918-1}}. < /ref >  was an [[Austria]]n composer and music historian of [[Czech people|Czech]] descent. \n \n == Life == \n He was born in [[M\u00fdto]], [[Rokycany District]], [[Bohemia]]. < ref name=Blom / >  His father was a cultured man, and his mother was the sister of [[Raphael Georg Kiesewetter]] < ref > Todd. R. Larry. (1991) ''Mendelssohn and His World'', Princeton University Press. p. 304. {{ISBN|0-691-02715-3}}. < /ref >  (1773\u20131850), the musical archaeologist and collector. Ambros studied at the [[Charles University of Prague|University of Prague]] < ref name=Blom / >  and was well-educated in music and the arts, which were his abiding passion. He was, however, destined for the law and an official career in the Austrian civil service, and he occupied various important posts under the ministry of justice, music being an [[avocation]]. < ref name= \" EB1911 \" > {{EB1911|inline=1|wstitle=Ambros, August Wilhelm|volume=1|page=798}} < /ref > \n \n From 1850 onwards, he became well known as a critic and essay-writer, and in 1860 he began working on his magnum opus, his ''History of Music'', which was published at intervals from 1862 < ref > [[Paul Henry Lang|Lang, Paul Henry]]. (1997) ''Music in Western Civilization'', W. W. Norton  &  Company. p. 987. {{ISBN|0-393-04074-7}}. < /ref >  in five volumes, the last two (1878, 1882) being edited and completed by [[Otto Kade]] and [[Wilhelm Langhans]]. < ref name= \" EB1911 \" / > \n \n Ambros was a professor of the history of music at [[Prague]] from 1869 to 1871. < ref > Meinong, Alexius  &  [[Guido Adler|Adler, Guido]] (1995) ''Eine Freundschaft in Briefen Alexius'', Rodopi. p. 8. {{ISBN|90-5183-867-0}}. < /ref >  Also in Prague, he sat on the board of governors in the [[Prague Conservatory|Prague Royal Conservatory]]. By 1872, he was living in Vienna and was employed by the Department of Justice as an officer and by [[Rudolf, Crown Prince of Austria|Prince Rudolf's]] family as his tutor. Through his work in Vienna, he was given a leave of absence for half the year in order to let him travel the world to collect musical information to include in his ''History of Music'' book. < ref name= \" Catholic \" > {{Cite Catholic Encyclopedia|wstitle=August Wilhelm Ambros}} < /ref >  He was an excellent pianist, and the author of numerous compositions reminiscent of [[Felix Mendelssohn]]. < ref name= \" EB1911 \" / > \n \n Ambros died at [[Vienna]], Austria at the age of 59. < ref name= \" Catholic \" / > \n \n ==Notes== \n {{Reflist}} \n \n {{Authority control}} \n \n {{Use dmy dates|date=November 2012}} \n \n {{DEFAULTSORT:Ambros, August Wilhelm}} \n [[Category:1816 births]] \n [[Category:1876 deaths]] \n [[Category:People from M\u00fdto]] \n [[Category:People from the Kingdom of Bohemia]] \n [[Category:19th-century Austrian musicians]] \n [[Category:19th-century classical composers]] \n [[Category:19th-century Czech musicians]] \n [[Category:Austrian classical composers]] \n [[Category:Austrian male classical composers]] \n [[Category:Austrian people of German Bohemian descent]] \n [[Category:Charles University alumni]] \n [[Category:Czech classical composers]] \n [[Category:Czech male classical composers]] \n [[Category:German Bohemian people]] \n [[Category:Czech Romantic composers]] \n [[Category:19th-century male musicians]]"
