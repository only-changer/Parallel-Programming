  "'''Alger of Li\u00e8ge''' (1055\u20131131), known also as Alger of Cluny and Algerus Magister, was a learned [[clergyman]] and canonist from [[Li\u00e8ge]], author of several notable works. \n \n Alger was first [[deacon]] and [[scholaster]] of [[St Bartholomew's Church, Li\u00e8ge|church of St Bartholomew]] in his native [[Li\u00e8ge]] and was then appointed ({{circa|1100}}) as a canon in [[St. Lambert's Cathedral, Li\u00e8ge|St. Lambert's Cathedral]]. Moreover, he acted as the personal secretary of bishop [[Otbert of Li\u00e8ge|Otbert]] from 1103.   < ref name =  \" DeJong \" > F.P.C. De Jong,  \" A Comparative Study of Schoolmasters in Eleventh Century Normandy and the Southern Low Countries \" , Ph.D. thesis, 2018 < /ref >  He declined offers from German bishops and finally retired to the monastery of [[Cluny]] after 1121, where he died at a high age, leaving behind a solid reputation for piety and intelligence. < ref name= \" EB1911 \" > {{EB1911|inline=y|wstitle=Alger of Li\u00e9ge|volume=1|page=642. }} This cites: \n * [[Jacques Paul Migne|Migne]], ''[[Patrologia Latina|Patrol Ser. Deutsch. < /ref > \n \n He played a leading role in the trial of [[Rupert of Deutz]] in 1116.  < ref name = \" DeJong \"   / > \n \n His ''History of the Church of Li\u00e8ge'', and many of his other works, are lost. The most important remaining are: \n #''De Misericordia et Justitia'' (On Mercy and Justice), a collection of biblical extracts and sayings of Church Fathers with commentary (an important work for the history of church law and discipline), which is to be found in the ''Anecdota'' of [[Edmond Mart\u00e8ne|Mart\u00e8ne]], vol. v. \n #''De Sacramentis Corporis et Sanguinis Domini''; a treatise, in three books, against the [[Berengar of Tours|Berengar]]ian heresy, highly commended by [[Peter of Cluny]] and [[Desiderius Erasmus|Erasmus]].
